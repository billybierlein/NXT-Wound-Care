### Home Care Instructions for Healing Pressure Ulcer

Hello! We’re here to support you on your healing journey. Taking care of your pressure ulcer at home is important, and we’re here to guide you every step of the way. Remember, healing takes time, and every small step you take helps you get closer to recovery. Let’s get started!

#### Daily Care Routine

**1. Clean the Wound:**
- **Supplies Needed:** Mild soap, warm water, clean washcloth, and clean towels.
- **Steps:**
  1. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water.
  2. Gently clean the area around the ulcer with a mild soap and warm water using a clean washcloth.
  3. Pat the area dry with a clean towel. Do not rub, as this can cause irritation.

**2. Apply Dressings:**
- **Supplies Needed:** Prescribed wound dressing, medical tape, and gloves.
- **Steps:**
  1. Wear gloves to maintain cleanliness.
  2. Carefully apply the prescribed dressing to the ulcer. Ensure it covers the entire wound.
  3. Secure the dressing with medical tape if needed.
  4. Change the dressing as directed by your healthcare provider, usually once a day or when it becomes wet or soiled.

**3. Relieve Pressure:**
- **Tips:**
  - Change your position every 1-2 hours to relieve pressure on the ulcer.
  - Use pillows or foam pads to cushion and support your body.
  - If you have a special mattress or cushion, use it to help distribute weight evenly.

**4. Promote Circulation:**
- **Tips:**
  - Gently move your arms and legs several times a day to encourage blood flow.
  - Elevate your legs when sitting to reduce swelling and improve circulation.
  - Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water unless otherwise advised by your doctor.

#### Important Safety Information

- **Watch for Signs of Infection:**
  - Increased redness, swelling, or warmth around the ulcer
  - Pus or foul-smelling drainage
  - Increased pain or tenderness
  - Fever or chills

- **When to Contact Your Healthcare Provider:**
  - If you notice any signs of infection
  - If the ulcer does not seem to be healing or appears to be getting worse
  - If you experience increased pain or discomfort
